Multi‑Omics Integration for Biomarker Discovery

We develop integrative models to identify robust biomarkers across omics layers. We compare late‑fusion, shared representation learning, and graph‑based approaches to align signals across cohorts.

Status: Active
Primary domain: Computational biology · Biomarkers
Contact: chenm19@wfu.edu

Goals

  • Normalize and batch‑correct omics tables; evaluate harmonization methods.
  • Train representation models (autoencoders, contrastive learning) to align modalities.
  • Validate biomarkers with cross‑cohort generalization and pathway enrichment.

Getting Started

  1. Audit data availability and licensing; prepare a data dictionary.
  2. Prototype embeddings with PCA/UMAP and autoencoders.
  3. Define robustness checks (bootstrapping, leave‑cohort‑out).
